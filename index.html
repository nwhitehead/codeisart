The shapes are simple, but there is real depth. I like the closely spaced curves defining circle edges. I also see a single straight line.

I interpret the colors here as LGBT coded, might be over-reaching.

Hexagonal (2022)
License: CC BY 4.0
Repository: djnavarro/series-hexagonal
Presented in part (2/99 images)

Smudged Hexagon 8 1549

I love the mix of precise geometry and organic smudging.

Algorithm is to start with precise geometric lines, divide them into sections, then perturb points with uniform randomness. The trick is dividing lines based on their length. This process is done iteratively, measuring segments after perturbation each time.

Smudged Hexagon 8 1545

Final algorithm calls the iterative smudging process multiple times in different contexts, draws polygons with transparency.

Fractal detail to me can be nice but is sometimes boring. This smudging is interesting, it doesn't have the same level of detail at every scale.

Bound (2023)
License: CC BY 4.0
Repository: djnavarro/series-bound
Presented in part (1/16 images)

Dreamlike 13 2170

I love the dusty texture, almost perfect but not perfect circular edges, and the almost reflective orb look here. To me this one looks like an eye, almost looking at me but actually looking just behind me. The texture and edges reminds me of airbrush spray.

This is a variation of Pollen algorithm. Points are transformed with random affine transform, then random choice of: normalize magnitude, take sin of all coords, take 2 sine of all coords. Afterwards, points are projected onto polygon and plotted. This is all iterated.
